Browsing the web Safely

First, it's important to understand that web security is all about protecting yourself and your personal information from hackers and other online threats. There are many steps you can take to stay safe online, such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious websites and emails, and keeping your software and operating system up to date.

To illustrate the breadth of information that web apps can access, here are six properties from Tables 5-10 and 5-11:

  1. Screen resolution: This property tells a web app the size of your screen, which can be used to optimize website layouts or display ads.
  2. Time zone: This property tells a web app your current time zone, which can be used to personalize content or track your location.
  3. Browser type and version: This property tells a web app what browser you're using, which can be used to optimize website features or deliver targeted ads.
  4. IP address: This property reveals your unique IP address, which can be used to track your location or identify your internet service provider.
  5. Installed fonts: This property tells a web app what fonts you have installed on your computer, which can be used to create unique fingerprinting profiles or deliver personalized content.
  6. Plug-ins installed: This property tells a web app what browser plug-ins you have installed, which can be used to identify your interests or vulnerabilities.

  1. Google Safety Center Google Safety - Google offers a range of tools and resources to help users stay safe online, including advice on safe browsing, keeping your accounts secure, and protecting your privacy.
  2. US-CERT US-CERT - The United States coomputer Emergency Readiness Team provides tips and advice on a variety of online security topics, including protecting your identity, securing your devices, and staying safe on social media.
